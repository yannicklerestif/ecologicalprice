Ecological Footprint

Ecological prices are based on the work of the Global Footprint Network.

Their work is based on the notion of global hectare, a unit in which they are able to express all resources we consume, and all available resources.

For CO2 emissions, it will be the amount of land needed to absorb them, for agricultural products, the land area needed to produce them, for livestock products, the land needed to feed them, and so on.

Different types of land account for a different number of global hectares depending on a coefficient they call Equivalence Factor.

They calculated the world's total biocapacity as the following:

World's Total Biocapacity: {{worldTotalBiocapacity | number:'.2-2'}} billion gha

Pricing Global Hectares

Price in International Dollars

To give an ecological price to a global hectare, we divide the amount of money spent in one year in the world by the world total biocapacity.

We can then compute the ecological price of an object by multiplying the number of global hectares needed to produce it by the ecological price of a global hectare.

To evaluate the amount of money spent in one year, the first idea that comes to mind is to use the world's GDP.

But the problem with this idea is that the ecological prices computed this way do not depend on the purchase power in a country. In other words, if the general level of the prices is a lot lower in a country, ecological prices in this country will be relatively a lot higher.

To take this into account, we use the world's GDP expressed in International Dollars as computed by the world bank, that takes the purchasing power parity into account. Its value is the following:

World's GDP in International Dollars: I${{worldPppGdp | number:'.2-2'}} billion

The price of a global hectare in International Dollars is then:

Ecological price of a global hectare: {{worldPppGdp | number:'.2-2'}} / {{worldTotalBiocapacity | number:'.2-2'}} = I${{globalHectarePriceIntDollars | number:'.2-2'}}

Because here the quantities we manipulate are typically a lot smaller than a hectare, we will use global square meters, that are 1 / {{10000 | number}} th of a global hectare. The ecological price of a global square meter is then:

Ecological Price of a global square meter: {{globalHectarePriceIntDollars | number:'.2-2'}} / {{10000 | number}} = I${{globalSquareMeterPriceIntDollars | number:'.2-3'}}

Price in {{selectedCurrency.name}} for {{selectedCountry.name}}

The price of a global square meter for a country is the computed by multiplying the price in International Dollars by the average level of the prices in this country. We then convert it into the chosen currency.

Prices level in a country are computed by dividing the PPP GDP of this country by its nominal GDP.

Average price level in {{selectedCountry.name}}: {{selectedCountry.avgPrices | number:'.2-2'}}
Exchange rate for {{selectedCurrency.name}}: {{selectedCurrency.exchangeRate | number:'.2-2'}}

Which gives us:

Price of a global square meter in {{selectedCountry.name}}: {{globalSquareMeterPriceIntDollars | number:'.2-3'}} * {{selectedCountry.avgPrices | number:'.2-2'}} * {{selectedCurrency.exchangeRate | number:'.2-2'}} = {{globalSquareMeterPriceSelectedCurrency | currency:selectedCurrency.code:'symbol':'.3-3'}}

Prices Of CO2 Objects

The ecological price of CO2 objects, by definition, only depends on the CO2 emitted to create them. The ecological footprint, in global hectares, of a CO2 object, is computed by multiplying its CO2 cost by the ecological footprint of CO2 emissions.

The ecological footprint of CO2 emissions is computed by the Global Footprint Network:

CO2 ecological footprint: {{co2Footprint * 10000}} gm² / kg

Then, for example for the CO2 object {{sampleCo2Object.name}}:

CO2 emissions for {{sampleCo2Object.name}}: {{sampleCo2Object.details.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} kg

The ecological footprint is then the following:

Ecological footprint for {{sampleCo2Object.name}}: {{co2Footprint * 10000}} * {{sampleCo2Object.details.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} = {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{sampleCo2Object.name}}: {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-2'}} * {{ globalSquareMeterPriceSelectedCurrency | number:'.2-3'}} = {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalPrice.value | currency:selectedCurrency.code}}

Prices Of Crop Objects

The ecological price of crop objects, by definition, only depends on the surface of land needed to produce them.

The surface of land needed to produce crop objects is obtained by dividing the weight of the object by the average yield of crops for this object. The yield of crops for crop object is provided by the FAO. For {{sampleCropObject.name}}, for example, the yield is:

Crop yield for {{sampleCropObject.name}}: {{sampleCropObject.details.objectYield | number:'.2-2'}} tons / ha
Or in kg / m²: {{sampleCropObject.details.objectYield | number:'.2-2'}} * 1000 / {{10000 | number}} = {{sampleCropObject.details.objectYield / 10 | number:'.2-3'}} kg / m²

We can then compute the land surface needed to produce it:

Surface of land needed to produce {{sampleCropObject.name}}: 1 / {{sampleCropObject.details.objectYield / 10 | number:'.2-3'}} = {{sampleCropObjectSurfaceNeeded | number:'.2-2'}} m²

Then, we multiply by the Equivalence Factor for crop land, provided by the Global Footprint Network:

Equivalence Factor for crop land: {{equivalenceFactorForCropLand | number:'.2-2'}}

This gives us the ecological footprint of the object:

Ecological footprint for {{sampleCropObject.name}}: {{equivalenceFactorForCropLand | number:'.2-2'}} * {{sampleCropObjectSurfaceNeeded | number:'.2-2'}} = {{sampleCropObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{sampleCropObject.name}}: {{sampleCropObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} * {{ globalSquareMeterPriceSelectedCurrency | number:'.2-3'}} = {{sampleCropObjectEcologicalPrice.value | currency:selectedCurrency.code}}

Prices Of Livestock Objects

The ecological price of livestock objects depends on the land surface needed to grow the products needed to feed them.

The Global Footprint Network provides the total ecological footprint and the total quantity produced for livestock objects.

For example, for {{sampleLivestockObject.name}}:

Total quantity produced: {{sampleLivestockObject.details.totalProduced | number}} tons
Total ecological footprint: {{sampleLivestockObject.details.totalEcologicalFootprint | number}} gha

We can then compute the ecological footprint necessary to produce 1 kg:

Ecological footprint for 1 kg:
 {{sampleLivestockObject.details.totalEcologicalFootprint | number}} / {{sampleLivestockObject.details.totalProduced | number}} * 1000 * {{10000 | number}} = {{sampleLivestockObjectEcologicalFootprint1Kg * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

This value is the ecological footprint for 1 kg of raw product, but we must account for losses when transforming it. To compute the ecological price of the final product, we must divide by the ratio between transformed product and raw product. For {{sampleLivestockObject.name}}, this value is:

Percentage of raw product remaining after transformation: {{sampleLivestockObject.details.retailCutPercent * 100}} %

We can now compute the ecological footprint of the object:

Ecological footprint for {{sampleLivestockObject.name}}: {{sampleLivestockObjectEcologicalFootprint1Kg * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} / {{sampleLivestockObject.details.retailCutPercent | number:'.2-2'}} = {{sampleLivestockObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{sampleLivestockObject.name}}: {{sampleLivestockObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} * {{ globalSquareMeterPriceSelectedCurrency | number:'.2-3'}} = {{sampleLivestockObjectEcologicalPrice.value | currency:selectedCurrency.code}}
