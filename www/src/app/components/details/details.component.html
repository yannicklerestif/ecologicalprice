Ecological Footprint

Ecological prices are based on the work of the Global Footprint Network.

Their work is based on the notion of global hectare, a unit in which they are able to express all resources we consume, and all available resources.

For CO2 emissions, it will be the amount of land needed to absorb them, for agricultural products, the land area needed to produce them, for livestock products, the land needed to feed them, and so on.

Different types of land account for a different number of global hectares depending on a coefficient they call Equivalence Factor.

They calculated the world's total biocapacity as the following:

World's Total Biocapacity: {{worldTotalBiocapacity | number:'.2-2'}} Mds gha

Pricing Global Hectares

Price in International Dollars

To give an ecological price to a global hectare, we divide the amount of money spent in one year in the world by the world total biocapacity.

We can then compute the ecological price of an object by multiplying the number of global hectares needed to produce it by the ecological price of a global hectare.

To evaluate the amount of money spent in one year, the first idea that comes to mind is to use the world's GDP.

But the problem with this idea is that the ecological prices computed this way do not depend on the purchase power in a country. In other words, if the general level of the prices is a lot lower in a country, ecological prices in this country will be relatively a lot higher.

To take this into account, we use the world's GDP expressed in International Dollars as computed by the world bank, that takes the purchasing power parity into account. Its value is the following:

World's GDP in International Dollars: I${{worldPppGdp | number:'.2-2'}} Mds

The price of a global hectare in International Dollars is then:

Price of a global hectare: {{worldPppGdp | number:'.2-2'}} / {{worldTotalBiocapacity | number:'.2-2'}} = I${{globalHectarePriceIntDollars | number:'.2-2'}}

Price in {{selectedCurrency.name}} for {{selectedCountry.name}}

The price of a global hectare for a country is the computed by multiplying the price in International Dollars by the average level of the prices in this country. We then convert it into the chosen currency.

Prices level in a country are computed by dividing the PPP GDP of this country by its nominal GDP.

Average price level in {{selectedCountry.name}}: {{selectedCountry.avgPrices | number:'.2-2'}}
Exchange rate for {{selectedCurrency.name}}: {{selectedCurrency.exchangeRate | number:'.2-2'}}

Which gives us:

Price of a global hectare in {{selectedCountry.name}}: {{globalHectarePriceIntDollars | number:'.2-2'}} * {{selectedCountry.avgPrices | number:'.2-2'}} * {{selectedCurrency.exchangeRate | number:'.2-2'}} = {{globalHectarePriceSelectedCurrency | currency:selectedCurrency.code}}

Prices Of CO2 Objects

The ecological price of CO2 objects, by definition, only depends on the CO2 emitted to create them. The ecological footprint, in global hectares, of a CO2 object, is computed by multiplying its CO2 cost by the ecological footprint of CO2 emissions.

The ecological footprint of CO2 emissions is computed by the Global Footprint Network:

CO2 ecological footprint: {{co2Footprint * 1000}} gha / ton

Then, for example for the CO2 object {{sampleCo2ObjectEpObject.name}}:

CO2 emissions for {{sampleCo2ObjectEpObject.name}}: {{sampleCo2Object.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} kg

The ecological footprint is then the following:

Ecological footprint for {{sampleCo2ObjectEpObject.name}}: {{co2Footprint * 1000}} * {{sampleCo2Object.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} = {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-7'}} gha

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global hectare as computed above:

Ecological price for {{sampleCo2ObjectEpObject.name}}: {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-7'}} * {{ globalHectarePriceSelectedCurrency | number:'.2-2'}} = {{sampleCo2ObjectEcologicalPrice.value | currency:selectedCurrency.code}}
