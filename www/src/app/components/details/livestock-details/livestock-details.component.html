Prices Of Livestock Objects

The ecological price of livestock objects depends on the land surface needed to grow the products needed to feed them.

The Global Footprint Network provides the total ecological footprint and the total quantity produced for livestock objects.

For example, for {{livestockObject.name}}:

Total quantity produced: {{livestockObject.details.totalProduced | number}} tons
Total ecological footprint: {{livestockObject.details.totalEcologicalFootprint | number}} gha

We can then compute the ecological footprint necessary to produce 1 kg:

Ecological footprint for 1 kg: {{livestockObject.details.totalEcologicalFootprint | number}} / {{livestockObject.details.totalProduced | number}} x 1000 x {{10000 | number}} = {{livestockObjectFootprint1Kg * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

This value is the ecological footprint for 1 kg of raw product, but we must account for losses when transforming it. To compute the ecological price of the final product, we must divide by the ratio between transformed product and raw product. For {{livestockObject.name}}, this value is:

Percentage of raw product remaining after transformation: {{livestockObject.details.retailCutPercent * 100}} %

We can now compute the ecological footprint of the object:

Ecological footprint for {{livestockObject.name}}: {{livestockObjectFootprint1Kg * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} / {{livestockObject.details.retailCutPercent | number:'.2-2'}} = {{livestockObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{livestockObject.name}}: {{livestockObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} x {{ globalSquareMeterPrice.value | number:'.2-3'}} = {{livestockObjectPrice | ecologicalPrice}}
