Prices Of Crop Objects

The ecological price of crop objects, by definition, only depends on the surface of land needed to produce them.

The surface of land needed to produce crop objects is obtained by dividing the weight of the object by the average yield of crops for this object. The yield of crops for crop object is provided by the FAO. For {{cropObject.name}}, for example, the yield is:

Crop yield for {{cropObject.name}}: {{cropObject.details.objectYield | number:'.2-2'}} tons / ha
Or in kg / m²: {{cropObject.details.objectYield | number:'.2-2'}} x 1000 / {{10000 | number}} = {{cropObject.details.objectYield / 10 | number:'.2-3'}} kg / m²

We can then compute the land surface needed to produce it:

Surface of land needed to produce {{cropObject.name}}: 1 / {{cropObject.details.objectYield / 10 | number:'.2-3'}} = {{cropObjectSurfaceNeeded | number:'.2-2'}} m²

Then, we multiply by the Equivalence Factor for crop land, provided by the Global Footprint Network:

Equivalence Factor for crop land: {{equivalenceFactorForCropLand | number:'.2-2'}}

This gives us the ecological footprint of the object:

Ecological footprint for {{cropObject.name}}: {{equivalenceFactorForCropLand | number:'.2-2'}} x {{cropObjectSurfaceNeeded | number:'.2-2'}} = {{cropObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{cropObject.name}}: {{cropObject.ef * 10000 | number:'.2-2'}} x {{ globalSquareMeterPrice.value | number:'.2-3'}} = {{cropObjectPrice | ecologicalPrice}}
