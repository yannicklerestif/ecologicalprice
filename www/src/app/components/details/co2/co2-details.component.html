Prices Of CO2 Objects

The ecological price of CO2 objects, by definition, only depends on the CO2 emitted to create them. The ecological footprint, in global hectares, of a CO2 object, is computed by multiplying its CO2 cost by the ecological footprint of CO2 emissions.

The ecological footprint of CO2 emissions is computed by the Global Footprint Network:

CO2 ecological footprint: {{co2Footprint * 10000}} gm² / kg

Then, for example for the CO2 object {{co2Object.name}}:

CO2 emissions for {{co2Object.name}}: {{co2Object.details.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} kg

The ecological footprint is then the following:

Ecological footprint for {{co2Object.name}}: {{co2Footprint * 10000}} x {{co2Object.details.co2Cost | number:'.2-2'}} = {{co2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-2'}} gm²

We can then compute the ecological price by multiplying the ecological price by the price of a global square meter computed above:

Ecological price for {{co2Object.name}}: {{co2ObjectEcologicalFootprint | number:'.2-2'}} x {{ globalSquareMeterPrice.value | number:'.2-3'}} = {{co2ObjectPrice | ecologicalPrice}}
